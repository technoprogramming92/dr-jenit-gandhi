Laparoscopic Duodenal Switch

Laparoscopic Duodenal Switch (DS), also known as Biliopancreatic Diversion with Duodenal Switch (BPD/DS), is a complex bariatric surgery that combines both restrictive and malabsorptive approaches to achieve significant long-term weight loss. This procedure involves reducing the size of the stomach and rerouting a lengthy portion of the small intestine, creating a more significant bypass compared to other forms of gastric surgeries.

Eligibility for Laparoscopic Duodenal Switch

  • BMI Requirements: Typically reserved for patients with a BMI of 50 or higher, or those with a BMI of 40 or more who have not achieved sufficient weight loss with other surgeries.
  • Health Conditions: Particularly effective for patients with severe metabolic conditions such as diabetes and hyperlipidemia.
  • Lifestyle Commitment: Candidates must be prepared for substantial lifelong changes in their eating habits and must adhere to strict dietary guidelines to avoid nutritional deficiencies and complications.

Required Preparations before Surgery

  • Comprehensive Evaluations: Includes extensive medical examinations, nutritional counseling, and psychological assessments to ensure patients are fully prepared for both the surgery and its lifelong implications.
  • Preoperative Diet: Patients are typically placed on a strict diet to reduce liver size and minimize surgical risks.
  • Medication Adjustments: Necessary changes in medication regimens are made under medical supervision to prepare for surgery.

The Surgical Procedure

Performed laparoscopically, the Duodenal Switch surgery involves:

  • Sleeve Gastrectomy: Approximately 70% of the stomach is removed, leaving a banana-shaped stomach that limits food intake.
  • Intestinal Bypass: A significant portion of the small intestine is bypassed, dramatically reducing the absorption of calories and nutrients, particularly fats.

What to Expect After Surgery

  • Hospital Stay: Recovery in the hospital typically lasts about 2 to 5 days.
  • Diet Progression: Patients start with a liquid diet, advancing to pureed foods, and eventually, solid foods over a period of several weeks.
  • Lifelong Follow-up: Regular follow-up visits are crucial to monitor weight loss, nutritional status, and manage any potential complications.

Expected Results from Laparoscopic Duodenal Switch

Patients can expect to lose up to 70-90% of their excess body weight within the first two years post-operation. The procedure is highly effective in resolving or improving type 2 diabetes, sleep apnea, and other metabolic syndromes.

Potential Risks and Complications

  • Nutritional Deficiencies: Due to the extensive intestinal bypass, patients require lifelong nutritional supplements, including vitamins and minerals.
  • Protein Malnutrition and Anemia: These are common issues due to the reduced absorption capabilities of the shortened digestive tract.
  • Surgical Complications: Potential risks include leaks from staple lines, infections, and complications from anesthesia.
