Biliary Stricture

Biliary stricture refers to the narrowing of the bile ducts, which can obstruct the flow of bile from the liver to the small intestine. This condition can result from various causes, including injury, inflammation, or tumors, and can lead to serious health complications if not treated. Dr. Jenit Gandhi, the best biliary surgeon in Ahmedabad, specializes in the diagnosis and surgical treatment of biliary strictures, offering advanced techniques to restore normal bile flow and improve patient outcomes. Consulting a biliary surgery doctor in Ahmedabad ensures that patients receive top-notch care and effective treatment.

Understanding Biliary Stricture:

Bile ducts are responsible for transporting bile, a digestive fluid produced by the liver, to the small intestine. When these ducts become narrowed or blocked, it can lead to bile buildup in the liver, causing jaundice, infections, and liver damage. Biliary stricture treatment is essential to avoid further complications. Common causes of biliary strictures include previous surgery, chronic inflammation, pancreatitis, and certain cancers. Biliary stricture surgery is often the most effective approach for resolving these issues.

Symptoms of Biliary Stricture:

  • Jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes)
  • Abdominal pain, particularly in the upper right quadrant
  • Fever and chills, indicating potential infection
  • Itching (pruritus) due to bile salt accumulation in the skin
  • Dark urine and pale stools

Diagnosis:

Diagnosing a biliary stricture involves a combination of medical history, physical examination, and imaging studies. Dr. Gandhi, a top biliary surgery doctor in Ahmedabad, may recommend the following diagnostic tests:

  • Ultrasound: To visualize the bile ducts and detect any narrowing or blockages.
  • Magnetic Resonance Cholangiopancreatography (MRCP): A non-invasive imaging technique that provides detailed images of the bile ducts.
  • Endoscopic Retrograde Cholangiopancreatography (ERCP): A procedure that combines endoscopy and X-ray imaging to diagnose and treat biliary strictures.
  • Liver function tests: To assess the extent of liver damage due to bile duct obstruction.

Treatment Options:

The treatment for biliary strictures depends on the underlying cause and severity of the condition. Biliary stricture treatment is often achieved through various surgical options:

  • Endoscopic Stent Placement: A minimally invasive procedure where a stent is placed inside the bile duct to keep it open and allow bile to flow freely.
  • Balloon Dilatation: This procedure involves inflating a balloon inside the narrowed bile duct to widen the stricture.
  • Surgical Bypass: In cases where the stricture cannot be treated endoscopically, biliary stricture surgery may be performed to reroute the bile flow around the blockage.
  • Resection of Stricture: For strictures caused by tumors or scar tissue, surgical removal of the affected portion of the bile duct may be necessary.

Postoperative Care:

After biliary stricture surgery, close monitoring and follow-up are essential to ensure successful recovery. Dr. Gandhi will provide detailed instructions on wound care, diet, and activity restrictions. Regular follow-up appointments will be scheduled to monitor bile flow and liver function.
