Intra Gastric Balloon Insertion

Intragastric balloon insertion is a non-surgical, reversible treatment in which a saline-filled silicon balloon is endoscopically placed into the stomach and left in place for 6 months to a year. The balloon takes up the majority of the space in the stomach, leaving little room for food. On average, intragastric balloon surgery results in approximately 30% excess weight loss. It also improves various obesity-related co-morbidities such as type 2 diabetes, hypertension, and high cholesterol.

Eligibility for Intra Gastric Balloon Insertion

  • Intragastric balloon insertion procedure is intended for people who want to shed 10 to 15 kg of body weight. Balloon therapy is an appropriate alternative for overweight people and those with grade 1 obesity, particularly if they have failed to lose weight with other weight loss methods.
  • Balloon insertion is also a viable alternative for extremely obese patients who are unable to undergo anesthesia due to their weight. This surgery can be offered as a first stage, allowing patients to lose weight and become fit for anaesthesia at a later period.

Required Preparations before Intra Gastric Balloon Insertion

  • The first step is a consultation with the doctor and team to determine eligibility for this surgery.
  • Once we have decided to proceed with balloon insertion surgery, you will be required to complete a battery of tests. Depending on your clinical profile and co-morbidities, you may also need to consult a diabetologist, endocrinologist, nephrologist, or chest physician.
  • You will also need to follow a liquid diet for at least 48 hours before the surgery.
  • You will need to fast for 6 hours before the surgery.
  • Intra-gastric balloon insertion surgery is often performed as an outpatient procedure, and hospitalization is rarely required.

What to expect after Balloon Insertion

  • Balloon insertion surgery is an outpatient procedure, and you will usually be able to leave within 2 - 3 hours.
  • You will be on a liquid diet for approximately one week following balloon placement.
  • Most patients tolerate the balloon well. This technique is predicted to cause varying degrees of nausea and vomiting for around 7 to 10 days. The team will prescribe drugs to alleviate these symptoms, which may occasionally be severe. In this instance, you may be asked to be admitted to the hospital for a few days.
  • These symptoms are expected to subside after two weeks, and after that, the balloon normally causes no problems and patients feel comfortable.

Expected weight loss after Intra Gastric Balloon Insertion surgery

  • Patients may shed up to 30% of their excess weight after this intragastric balloon insertion. Weight loss is affected by a variety of factors, including the patient's baseline weight, age, gender, associated co-morbidities, the type of balloon inserted, the patient's behavioral adjustment, and so on.
  • Patients who embrace the lifestyle change and follow the team's behavioral modification instructions perform better following any weight loss operation, including balloon insertion surgery.
  • Please keep in mind that weight loss operations should not be viewed as a quick fix, but rather as a long-term commitment from both the doctor and the patient. To acquire the greatest results, you must follow the diet advised by the experts and exercise for at least 30 to 45 minutes per day.

Improvement in Co Morbidities

  • Balloon insertion operation results in 25 to 30% excess weight loss over 6 months to a year, depending on the type of balloon used.
  • Type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, high cholesterol, gout, PCOD, and other related conditions have all improved significantly.

Removal of Intra Gastric balloon and Maintenance of weight loss after it's removal

  • Depending on the type of balloon used, an intragastric balloon can remain in place for 6 months to a year.
  • Removal is also performed endoscopically and typically takes 20 minutes.
  • Once the balloon is removed, there is no restriction and a tendency to gain weight. It is recommended that you stay in touch with the team and stick to your diet and exercise routine even after the balloon is removed.

Downsides of Intra Gastric Balloon Insertion

  • Patients may experience varied degrees of nausea and vomiting following balloon insertion surgery. This usually settles down within a week or 15 days. Most of the time, hospitalization is unnecessary, and these symptoms can be treated as outpatients. Some people may develop increased acid reflux following this surgery.
  • Very rarely, the balloon gets inflated within the stomach. Don't panic!
  • The balloon is filled with dye-stained saline, and if any of it leaks, you will begin to pass blue urine. Please contact the doctor and team promptly, and they will schedule a balloon removal as soon as possible.
  • Intragastric balloon insertion surgery is a reversible weight loss method. After the balloon is removed, there may be a propensity to regain weight. This can be reduced if you maintain a rigorous diet and exercise routine after balloon removal. Please keep in mind that the balloon is only a tool to help you shed the resistive weight.
