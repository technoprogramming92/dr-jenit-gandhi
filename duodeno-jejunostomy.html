Laparoscopic Sleeve Gastrectomy With Duodeno-jejunostomy

Laparoscopic Sleeve Gastrectomy with Duodeno-Jejunostomy (LSG-DJ) is an advanced bariatric surgery combining sleeve gastrectomy with a bypass of the upper part of the small intestine. This procedure not only reduces the size of the stomach to limit food intake but also alters nutrient absorption, significantly enhancing weight loss and metabolic outcomes.

Eligibility for Laparoscopic Sleeve Gastrectomy with Duodeno-Jejunostomy

  • BMI Requirements: Ideal for patients with a BMI of 40 or higher, or those with a BMI of 35 or more accompanied by serious health issues like diabetes, cardiovascular diseases, or severe sleep apnea.
  • Prior Weight Loss Efforts: Targeted at individuals who have unsuccessfully tried to lose weight through diet, exercise, or other medical treatments.
  • Commitment to Comprehensive Lifestyle Change: Candidates must be ready to make substantial, permanent changes to their dietary habits and physical activity levels.

Required Preparations before Surgery

  • Comprehensive Medical Review: Includes detailed physical examinations, nutritional assessments, and psychological screenings to ensure readiness for surgery.
  • Dietary Modifications: A specific pre-operative diet designed to decrease body fat, particularly in the liver, to facilitate a safer surgical procedure.
  • Medication and Lifestyle Adjustments: Patients may need to temporarily modify or halt the intake of certain medications. Smoking cessation is strongly recommended to improve recovery outcomes.

The Surgical Procedure

The procedure is performed under general anesthesia using a laparoscope. It involves:

  • Sleeve Gastrectomy: Approximately 80% of the stomach is removed, creating a tube or "sleeve" which limits food intake.
  • Duodeno-Jejunostomy: The first part of the small intestine (duodenum) is bypassed, and a connection is made between the end of the stomach (sleeve) and a lower part of the small intestine (jejunum). This reduces calorie absorption.

What to Expect After Surgery

  • Hospital Stay: Generally, patients are required to stay in the hospital for 2-3 days to ensure safe recovery and effective pain management.
  • Postoperative Diet: Begins with a liquid diet followed by gradual introduction of pureed foods, and eventually, solid foods over several weeks.
  • Ongoing Care: Regular follow-ups are crucial to monitor weight loss, dietary adherence, and general health.

Expected Results from Laparoscopic Sleeve Gastrectomy with Duodeno-Jejunostomy

Patients often experience significant weight loss, typically 60-80% of excess body weight within a year. The surgery also leads to marked improvements in metabolic disorders such as type 2 diabetes, hypertension, and hyperlipidemia.

Potential Risks and Complications

  • Nutritional Deficiencies: The alteration of the digestive system necessitates lifelong nutritional supplementation.
  • Gastrointestinal Issues: Potential for bile reflux, gastritis, and symptoms of indigestion.
