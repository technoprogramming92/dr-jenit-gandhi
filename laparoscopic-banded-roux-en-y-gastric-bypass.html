Laparoscopic Banded Roux-en Y Gastric Bypass

Laparoscopic Banded Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass (LRYGB-B) combines the standard Roux-en-Y procedure with the placement of a restrictive band around the newly created stomach pouch. This procedure not only limits the amount of food the stomach can hold but also slows its passage, enhancing the feeling of fullness and extending satiety. The laparoscopic banded Roux-en-Y gastric bypass procedure in Ahmedabad is a preferred solution for effective weight loss and metabolic improvement.

Eligibility for Laparoscopic Banded Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass

  • BMI Requirements: Ideal candidates have a BMI of 40 or more, or a BMI of 35 or more with serious weight-related health issues like type 2 diabetes or heart disease.
  • Previous Weight Loss Efforts: Suitable for individuals who have not achieved or maintained adequate clinically significant weight loss based on medical interventions.
  • Commitment to Change: Candidates must demonstrate a readiness to adopt a long-term lifestyle change, including diet and exercise, and follow post-operative medical advice.

Required Preparations before Banded Gastric Bypass Roux-en-Y Surgery

  • Medical Assessment: Includes comprehensive evaluations with a multidisciplinary team to ensure the suitability for surgery.
  • Nutritional Counseling: Patients receive detailed guidance on pre- and post-operative diets to prepare for and adapt to the changes in their digestive system.
  • Lifestyle Adjustments: Patients are often required to stop smoking and may need to adjust the intake of certain medications and substances pre-operatively.

The Surgical Procedure

The banded gastric bypass Roux-en-Y surgery in Ahmedabad involves making several small incisions to facilitate the laparoscopic instruments. The surgeon creates a small pouch at the top of the stomach and places a band around this pouch to restrict its expansion. The small intestine is then rerouted to this pouch, bypassing a significant portion of the stomach and small intestine.

What to Expect After Laparoscopic Banded Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass

  • Hospital Stay: Typically requires a stay of 1 to 3 days in the hospital for monitoring.
  • Dietary Progression: Begins with liquids, progresses to pureed foods, and then to solid foods over several weeks.
  • Ongoing Monitoring: Regular follow-ups are necessary to monitor weight loss, nutritional intake, and overall health.

Expected Results from Banded Roux-en-Y Surgery

Most patients can expect to lose 70-80% of their excess body weight within two years post-surgery. The laparoscopic banded Roux-en-Y gastric bypass procedure also contributes to the resolution or improvement of many obesity-related conditions.

Potential Risks and Complications

  • Nutritional Deficiencies: Due to reduced food intake and absorption, lifelong supplementation of vitamins and minerals is required.
  • Complications from the Band: Includes slippage or erosion, which may necessitate additional interventions.
  • Common Surgical Risks: Such as infections, bleeding, or adverse reactions to anesthesia.
